Life Has a Way of Pointing People Towards Their Destinies

Lauryn Sulani, a nurse and founder of the Centre for Down syndrome, Sickle Cell and Autism (Cedossa), was drawn to her mission through personal experience.

The Beginning of Cedossa

Lauryn founded Cedossa in 2021 after her child was diagnosed with Down syndrome. This organization was registered in September 2022 as a non-governmental charity...

Her Vision for Cedossa

Lauryn’s mission is to ensure individuals with Down syndrome and autism are accepted in society, advocating for their human rights and providing them with skills and empowerment.

Challenges and Achievements

Despite facing numerous challenges, Lauryn has helped over 120 families and continues to inspire change through Cedossa’s efforts in advocacy, awareness, and providing necessary resources.

Looking Forward

Lauryn’s plans include researching Down syndrome and autism prevalence in Malawi and working on influencing government policies for better inclusivity.
